History

The city was founded in 1662 Cracow castellan, then a full crown hetman Andrzej Potocki (from a known type of Polish magnates), and was named Stanislau - in honor of his father, the great crown hetman Stanislaw "Revere" Potocki [3]. The city was founded as a fortress to protect against the raids of Crimean Tatars and Zaporozhye Cossacks, and a strong point of Polish magnates Potocki in Galicia and received Magdeburg Law. In 1676-1677 gg. He withstood a siege by the Turkish troops. Stanislawow began with the Market Square and the Town Hall, which in a modified state is intact to this day. In its further development are reflected urban ideas of the Baroque "ideal city", embodied in the planning of the central part of the city, its streets, temples. For the original architecture of the city is sometimes called "small Lviv".
